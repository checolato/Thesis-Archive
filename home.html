Glossary

  • Familiarity
    The gradual accumulation of sensory knowledge that forms a person’s internal map of Taoyuan. Humidity pockets, pond reflections, and ecological sounds become learned cues that guide memory, comfort, and daily movement.
  • Sensory Data
    Environmental signals like water vapor, temperature drift, reflection intensity, sound textures captured around ponds. These data layers form the atmospheric foundation of the digital twin, allowing it to represent more than geometry.
  • Embodied Mapping
    A spatial understanding formed through moving, sensing, and noticing environmental shifts. Around Taoyuan’s ponds, embodied mapping emerges as loops, shortcuts, shaded edges, and seasonal routines shaped by water-driven microclimates.
  • Asymmetrical Presence
    The uneven visibility of humans and ponds across physical and digital space. Ponds may be disappearing from the city yet become central in the twin; human presence may be subtle in the landscape but heavily recorded through sensory traces.
  • Agent Behavior
    The simulated actions of digital inhabitants responding to pond-driven cues. Humidity preferences, memory hotspots, sound comfort, and light changes influence how agents navigate and learn the virtual environment.
